About Me

I'm an aspiring data analyst with a love of mathematics and an instinct to dive deep into data. With proficiency in SQL, Power BI, Python, and Excel, I have the skills to investigate data in any environment, and I strive for precision, clarity, and integrity in all facets of my work.

My aptitude for numbers and pattern recognition drove me to pursue a bachelor's in mathematics, while my love of knowledge and learning inspired me to study education and teaching. My journey to find new numbers to work with brought me to my current role in the operations industry, where after only three months, I improved my department's operation speed and data quality by 80%, designed several data cleaning programs, and provided KPI analysis of existing and in- development automation systems for company-wide use.

I consider myself a lifelong learner, so I love to explore new interests and hone new skills. I study statistics, data science, and analytics best practices every day, and I'm interested in learning more about machine learning algorithms and neural networks as they become essential industry tools. Even in my free time, I can't resist researching the best strategies for my favorite board games and video games, and I'm always on the lookout for new and interesting data to study in my next personal project.

My Projects

PGE Production Cost Model

In this project, I use Excel to create a production cost model of the Portland General Electric power system. Using data from the EIA, the NERC, and Portland General Electric, I find the range of demand on PGE's generating units, create a table of effective load for the combined system, determine the total annual hours of operation, and calculate the final cost of PGE's energy production.

Educational Attainment Analysis

In this project, I use SQL and Power BI to analyze educational attainment information at the county level. Using data from the USDA and the US Census, I compare graduation rates with rural/urban classification, geographical region, and school district spending to identify relationships to education levels.

Minnesota Energy Analysis

In this project, I use Jupyter Notebooks to perform exploratory analysis on energy use data in Minnesota to determine the success of the state's clean energy policies. Using data pulled from the EIA API, I examine changes in energy consumption, plant production capability, and CO2 emissions over 60 years.

Pokémon Game Analysis

In this project, I use SQL and Jupyter Notebooks to perform exploratory analysis on data and statistics from the popular video game series Pokémon. Using data pulled from public video game aggregate sites Serebii.net and Bulbapedia, I examine the effectiveness of different Pokemon by looking at base stats, type, height and weight, gender, catch rate, and experience group.
